About the Band

The Kooks are an English pop rock band formed in 2004 in Brighton. The band currently consists of Luke Pritchard (vocals/rhythm guitar), Hugh Harris (lead guitar/synth), Alexis Nunez (drums), and Peter Denton (bass guitar). A self-described "pop" band, their music is primarily influenced by the 1960s British Invasion movement and post-punk revival of the new millennium. With songs described as "catchy as hell", the Kooks have experimented in several genres including rock, Britpop, pop, reggae, ska, and more recently, funk and hip-hop, being described once as a "more energetic Thrills.

Biographies

Luke Pritchard

He and three of his friends formed The Kooks on a whim, paying much attention to their image through their clothing and hats. Three months after forming his band, Pritchard and The Kooks were signed to Virgin Records. He was born in Worthing, England on March 2nd 1985. His musical influence is mostly Bob Dylan. He was educated at Bedales School in Petersfield, Hampshire. Dated Mischa Barton, but they broke up and remained friends.

Hugh Harris

Born 19 July 1987 in Brighton, England. Hugh is an original member of the Kooks, he met Luke pritchard and former member Paul Garred as a student at the BRIT school in croydon. The first song he and The Kooks ever played together was a cover of The Strokes song "Reptilia."

Peter Denton

Born on september 22nd 1989, in England. He's a bassist who has been a member of the rock band The Kooks beginning in 2008. He replaced Max Rafferty after he was fired from the band for alleged drug abuse. He has been married to model Portia Freeman and together they have a son named Dylan. Some of his favourite artists are Cream, Pink Floyd and Michael Jackson.

Alexis Nunez

Otherwise known as Robden Alexis Nunez. He joined the band in 2012 as a replacement drummer, then signed up permanently. Doesn't like the limelight much, information on the drummer is scarce. Previously a member of 'Golden silvers'.
